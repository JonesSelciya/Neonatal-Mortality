PELVIC FLOOR ACTIVATION

You may have heard about the importance of pelvic floor health during and after pregnancy. The pelvic floor is a group of skeletal muscles at the base of the pelvis, that forms a bowl-shaped structure connecting our sit bones, our tailbone, and our pubic bone. In other words, it covers the entire bottom of our pelvis


          1.Identify the pelvic floor muscles by stopping urination midstream.
          2.Find a comfortable position to perform the exercises.
          3.Contract the pelvic floor muscles by squeezing and lifting them upward.
          4.Hold the contraction for 3-5 seconds, gradually increasing to 10 seconds.
          5.Relax the muscles and rest for 5-10 seconds between contractions.
          6.Repeat the contractions 10-15 times in one session, aiming for three sessions daily.
          7.Progress by increasing the duration or repetitions over time.
          8.Consistency is key; make pelvic floor exercises a regular part of your routine for best results.

STEP BACK

Step one foot back as if you are mid-step and lower your back heel down, leaning your body forward to create one long diagonal line through the crown of your head and down through the back heel, arms reaching back behind you. Keeping your back leg straight, lift the heel high, squeeze the back glute and push through the ball of the foot to engage your glutes while reaching your arms overhead. Feel a huge stretch on the hip flexor which tends to get very tight from pregnancy. From there, return to start position


          1.Identify Pelvic Floor Muscles: Locate by stopping urination or imagining lifting them upward.
          2.Choose a Position: Lie down, sit, or stand comfortably.
          3.Relax: Take deep breaths to relax your body.
          4.Contract Muscles: Squeeze and lift pelvic floor muscles.
          5.Hold Contraction: Start with 3-5 seconds, gradually increase to 10 seconds.
          6.Relax: Slowly release the contraction.
          7.Rest: Take 5-10 seconds rest between contractions.
          8.Repeat: Aim for 10-15 repetitions per session.
          9.Frequency: Perform at least three sessions daily.
          10.Progression: Increase intensity over time.
          11.Consistency: Make it a daily habit.

INTERNAL 45 WITH SIDE STRETCH

From your internal stagger position, drop the hand weights and stretch your back leg. Reach the arm of your extended back leg straight forward in line with your shoulder and bend the other elbow out to the side at a 90 degree angle. Reach your bent arm up overhead while allowing your hips to shift to the side, feeling an elongation from your hip all the way up through your fingertips, allowing breath to move into the sides of your body.


          1.Seated position: Start in a seated position on the floor with knees bent and feet flat.
          2.Engage core: Tighten your core muscles to stabilize your spine and maintain balance.
          3.Lean back slightly: Gently lean back while keeping your back straight, avoiding rounding of the spine.
          4.Rotate torso: Slowly rotate your torso to one side, aiming to bring your elbow toward the opposite knee.
          5.Side stretch: Feel the stretch along the side of your body as you rotate.
          6.Return to center: Gradually return to the starting position with control.
          7.Alternate sides: Repeat the movement on the other side, rotating to the opposite direction.
          8.Breathing: Inhale as you rotate, exhale as you return to the starting position.
          9.Focus on form: Maintain proper alignment throughout the exercise to prevent strain or injury.
          10.Start with light movements: Begin with small rotations and gradually increase range of motion
          as you become more comfortable with the exercise.

HAMSTRING PRESS

Come to a side-lying position on a prenatal wedge or a stack of pillows to keep your heart above your baby. Bend your knees so they are in 90-degree angles, knees in line with hips and feet in line with knees. Lift your top leg and flex your foot and then extend the leg straight back behind you, working into the back of the leg and glute.


          1.Lie on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor.
          2.Lift your hips toward the ceiling while keeping your shoulders on the ground.
          3.Extend one leg straight out, keeping hips level.
          4.Hold for a moment, then return to starting position.
          5.Repeat with the other leg.
          6.Aim for 8-12 repetitions on each side.
